Vanguard Chess

How to Play

The Setups

  • Standard: Solid wall of Checkers on Row 3, Pawns on Row 2, Chess on Row 1.
  • True Spacing: Solves gridlock by placing Checkers only on alternating squares (dark or light).
  • Inverted Vanguard: A highly aggressive variant where Checkers hold the back row, the King and Majors are on the 2nd row, and Pawns are on the 3rd row!

Checker Mechanics

  • Movement: Checkers (⬤) move 1 square diagonally forward into an empty space.
  • Jumping (Capturing): Checkers capture by jumping over an adjacent enemy piece into an empty square diagonally behind it.
  • Multi-Jumps: You can chain multiple jumps in a single turn! Click the next jump target, or click "End Jump" to stop early. Jumping is optional.
  • Checker Kings: Reaching the furthest rank promotes a Checker to a Checker King (❂), which can move and jump diagonally backward as well.

Chess Mechanics

  • Chess pieces move and capture completely normally via displacement.
  • Pawns move 1 square forward, and capture 1 square diagonally forward. (No double-move on first turn).
  • Victory: Capture the enemy Chess King to win! (Watch out: Multi-jumps make Kings uniquely vulnerable!)
